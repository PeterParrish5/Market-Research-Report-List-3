<p><p>Chromogenic materials refer to substances that change color in response to certain stimuli. There are three main types of chromogenic materials: photochromic, thermochromic, and electrochromic. Photochromic materials change color when exposed to light, typically UV light. Thermochromic materials change color with variations in temperature. Electrochromic materials change color when an electric current is applied. These materials are widely used in clothing, eyewear, smart windows, and sensors due to their ability to adapt and respond to different environmental conditions.</p></p>
