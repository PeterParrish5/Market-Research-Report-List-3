<p><p>Cellulosic fibers can be categorized into two main types: Natural Cellulose Fibers and Man-made Cellulose Fibers. Natural cellulose fibers, such as cotton, hemp, and jute, are derived from plants and are biodegradable and eco-friendly. On the other hand, Man-made Cellulose Fibers, like rayon, modal, and lyocell, are produced through chemical processes using natural cellulose as a raw material. These fibers provide similar properties to natural fibers but can be engineered for specific characteristics and functionalities. Both types of cellulosic fibers play a significant role in the textile industry.</p></p>
