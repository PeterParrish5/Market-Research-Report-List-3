<p><strong>The Dental Casting Porcelain Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>High Fusing</li><li>Medium Fusing</li><li>Low Fusing</li><li>Ultra-Low Fusing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dental casting porcelain market consists of different types based on their fusing temperatures. High fusing porcelain requires the highest temperature and is used in situations where durability is crucial, such as for dental bridges and crowns. Medium fusing porcelain is commonly used for inlays, onlays, and single crowns. Low fusing porcelain requires a lower temperature and is suitable for veneers and metal-ceramic restorations. Ultra-low fusing porcelain has the lowest temperature requirement and is typically used for small esthetic restorations like porcelain laminate veneers. These variations in fusing temperatures allow for different applications in dental procedures.</p></p>
