<p><p>Stationary nuclear air filtration systems are designed to be permanently installed in a specific location, such as a nuclear power plant or research facility. These systems are typically larger in size and have higher capacities for filtering out contaminants from the air. Portable nuclear air filtration systems, on the other hand, are designed to be easily moved from one location to another. They are commonly used for temporary or emergency situations, such as during nuclear accidents or decontamination efforts. Both types of systems play critical roles in maintaining air quality and safety in nuclear environments.</p></p>
