<p><p>Continuously Variable Transmissions (CVT) are a type of transmission that provides seamless shifting through a continuous range of gear ratios without distinct steps. There are two main types of CVT systems - Belt-Chain Drive CVT and Planetary CVT. </p><p>Belt-Chain Drive CVT uses a system of belts and pulleys to change the gear ratio, while Planetary CVT uses a set of planetary gears to achieve the same result. Both types offer advantages in terms of fuel efficiency and smooth acceleration, making them popular choices in the automotive industry.</p></p>
