<p><p>Lipid-lowering agents, also known as cholesterol-lowering agents or lipid-lowering drugs, refer to medications that are used to lower the levels of lipids, such as cholesterol and triglycerides, in the blood. These agents play a crucial role in managing various cardiovascular diseases, including hyperlipidemia, atherosclerosis, and other lipid-related disorders.</p><p>The lipid-lowering agent market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue its upward trajectory during the forecast period. One of the key factors driving this market growth is the increasing prevalence of lifestyle diseases, primarily obesity and cardiovascular disorders. Additionally, the rising geriatric population and sedentary lifestyles are further fueling the demand for lipid-lowering drugs.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in drug delivery systems, such as extended-release formulations and combination therapies, have improved the efficacy and convenience of lipid-lowering agents, thereby boosting their adoption. Patients' preference for non-invasive treatment options and increasing healthcare expenditure are also contributing factors to market growth.</p><p>In recent years, there have been several notable trends in the lipid-lowering agent market. One major trend is the growing popularity of statins, a class of lipid-lowering drugs that inhibit the enzyme responsible for cholesterol synthesis. Statins have become the first-line therapy for managing dyslipidemia and preventing cardiovascular events. Additionally, the market has witnessed the emergence of new drug classes, such as PCSK9 inhibitors and bile acid sequestrants, which offer alternative treatment options for patients who are intolerant to or do not respond well to statins.</p><p>In conclusion, the lipid-lowering agent market is expected to grow steadily at a CAGR of 13.1% during the forecast period.
