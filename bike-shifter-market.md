<p><ul><li>Mechanical</li><li>Electronic Shifting</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bike shifter market is divided into two types: Mechanical and Electronic Shifting. Mechanical shifting is operated by cables and relies on physical force from the rider to change gears. It is simple, durable, and cost-effective. On the other hand, Electronic Shifting uses a battery-powered electronic system to change gears with the push of a button. It offers precise and quick gear changes, but tends to be more expensive and may require more maintenance. Both types cater to different preferences and needs of cyclists.</p></p>
