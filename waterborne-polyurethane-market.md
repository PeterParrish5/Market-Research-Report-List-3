<p><p>Waterborne polyurethane market types include Anionic PUDs, Cationic PUDs, Non-Ionic PUDs, Component Polyurethane, Two-component Polyurethane, and Urethane-modified. Anionic PUDs have negatively charged particles, while Cationic PUDs have positively charged particles. Non-Ionic PUDs do not have charged particles. Component polyurethane is a single-component system, while two-component polyurethane requires mixing before application. Urethane-modified polyurethane contains additional urethane groups for enhanced performance. These market types offer a variety of options for different applications and performance requirements in the waterborne polyurethane industry.</p></p>
