<p><p>Digital printing sublimation ink is used in two main types of markets: Direct Sublimation and Transfer Sublimation. In Direct Sublimation, the ink is printed directly onto the substrate, such as fabric or ceramics, where it vaporizes under heat and pressure to create a permanent image. Transfer Sublimation involves printing the image onto a transfer paper, which is then transferred onto the substrate using heat and pressure. Both methods result in vibrant, long-lasting prints with excellent color reproduction and durability.</p></p>
