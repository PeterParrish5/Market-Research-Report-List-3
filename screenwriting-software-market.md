<p><p>The screenwriting software market can be categorized into two types: on-premise and cloud-based. On-premise software is installed and run on individual computers or local servers, requiring physical infrastructure and maintenance. In contrast, cloud-based software operates online and is accessible through internet browsers, eliminating the need for on-site installations. Cloud-based solutions provide scalability, flexibility, and collaboration features, enabling users to work remotely and share scripts easily. On-premise software offers greater control over data and security but can be limited in terms of accessibility and remote collaboration.</p></p>
