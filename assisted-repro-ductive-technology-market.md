<p><p>Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART) refers to the various medical procedures used to help couples conceive a child when they are facing fertility issues. The procedures involve manipulation of both eggs and sperm or the embryo itself to achieve a successful pregnancy. ART includes techniques like in vitro fertilization (IVF), intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI), and gamete intrafallopian transfer (GIFT), among others.</p><p>The Assisted Reproductive Technology Market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 9.8% during the forecast period. The key factors driving this growth include increasing infertility rates worldwide, advancement in technology, rising awareness, and growing social acceptance of ART.</p><p>One of the major trends observed in the market is the rising popularity of third-party assisted ART, such as sperm and egg donation, surrogacy, and embryo adoption. These options are particularly sought after by individuals or couples with infertility issues, same-sex couples, and single individuals who wish to have a child. The growing acceptance and legalization of surrogacy in various countries have further fueled the demand for ART services.</p><p>Moreover, the market is witnessing advancements in technology, such as the utilization of preimplantation genetic testing (PGT), which allows the identification of genetic abnormalities in embryos before implantation. PGT helps in reducing the risk of genetic disorders and helps couples make informed decisions about their reproductive choices.</p><p>Furthermore, the market has also witnessed the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) technologies, which aid in improving the success rates of ART procedures by analyzing various data points and providing personalized treatment plans based on individual patient characteristics.</p><p>Overall, the Assisted Reproductive Technology Market is expected to witness sustained growth due to increasing infertility rates, technological advancements, and the evolving landscape of societal acceptance and awareness.</p></p>

IUI involves placing sperm directly into the uterus during ovulation. ICI involves transferring sperm into the cervix. Other types may include surrogacy, donor eggs, and embryo cryopreservation. These technologies aid individuals and couples in overcoming fertility issues and achieving their reproductive goals.</p></p>
