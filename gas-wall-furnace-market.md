<p><p>Gas wall furnaces come in two main types: noncondensing and condensing. Noncondensing wall furnaces are the traditional type that exhausts hot combustion gases through a flue, while condensing wall furnaces are more energy-efficient as they extract heat from water vapor in the exhaust gases before expelling them. Noncondensing wall furnaces are more common and generally more affordable, while condensing wall furnaces are more efficient but may require a higher upfront cost. Both types are popular choices for heating homes efficiently and effectively.</p></p>
