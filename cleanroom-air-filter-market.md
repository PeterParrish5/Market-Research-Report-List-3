<p><p>Cleanroom air filters are categorized into two main types - HEPA (High Efficiency Particulate Air) filters and ULPA (Ultra Low Penetration Air) filters. HEPA filters are designed to capture particles as small as 0.3 microns, while ULPA filters are even more efficient, capturing particles as small as 0.12 microns. Both types of filters are essential in cleanroom environments to ensure the air is free from contaminants and maintain high levels of air quality for sensitive processes such as pharmaceutical manufacturing and electronics production.</p></p>
