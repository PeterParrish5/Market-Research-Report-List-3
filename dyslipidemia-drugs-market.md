<p><p>The dyslipidemia drugs market is categorized into different types based on the mechanism of action. Statins are the most commonly prescribed drugs that work by inhibiting an enzyme involved in cholesterol production, thereby reducing LDL cholesterol levels. Cholesterol absorption inhibitors are another type of medication that blocks the absorption of dietary cholesterol in the intestines. Other dyslipidemia drugs include fibrates, niacin, and PCSK9 inhibitors, which target different aspects of lipid metabolism to manage cholesterol levels. These drugs play a crucial role in treating dyslipidemia and preventing cardiovascular diseases.</p></p>
