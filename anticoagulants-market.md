<p><p>Anticoagulants are medications that prevent blood clotting, and they are classified into different market types. Platelet Aggregation Inhibitors (PAI) prevent platelets from sticking together, reducing the risk of clot formation. Low-Molecular-Weight Heparin (LMWH) inhibits clotting factors and is commonly used for preventing deep vein thrombosis. Direct Thrombin Inhibitors (DTI) and Direct Factor Xa Inhibitors (DFXa) directly target specific clotting factors. Vitamin K antagonists (VKA) interfere with the production of clotting factors. Other anticoagulants that do not fit into these categories exist in the market as well.</p></p>
