<p><p>Buoyancy materials are used to make objects float or stay afloat in water. There are different types of buoyancy materials, including chemical foam, hollow glass beads, and light composite materials. Chemical foam is made of lightweight materials that trap air bubbles to provide buoyancy. Hollow glass beads are lightweight and provide buoyancy by trapping air inside their hollow structure. Light composite materials are a combination of lightweight materials such as carbon fiber or fiberglass, providing buoyancy through their low density and high strength properties.</p></p>
