<p><ul><li>Stereolithography, SLA (Liquid Based)</li><li>Fused Deposition Modeling, FDM (Solid Based)</li><li>Selective Laser Sintering, SLS ( Powder Based)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Surgical 3D printing market is composed of different types based on the technology used. Stereolithography (SLA) is a liquid-based method that uses a laser to create solid objects by curing liquid resin. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) is a solid-based technique that melts a plastic filament and deposits it layer by layer. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) is a powder-based method where a laser fuses powdered material together to create the desired object. These technologies are used in the healthcare industry to create custom implants, surgical models, and instruments for better patient outcomes.</p></p>
