<p><p>Thermal Energy Storage (TES) is a technology that allows the storage of excess thermal energy to be used at a later time. It typically involves the conversion of excess electrical energy into thermal energy that can be stored in various forms such as ice or hot water. This stored thermal energy can then be used for heating or cooling purposes when demand is high or when renewable energy sources are unavailable.</p><p>The global market for Thermal Energy Storage is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. CSP plants use mirrors or lenses to concentrate sunlight onto a receiver, which then transfers the heat to a TES system. This integration allows for the storage of excess solar thermal energy for later use, thus providing continuous power generation.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the development of innovative storage materials to enhance the efficiency of TES systems. <p><strong>The Thermal Energy Storage Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Sensible Heat Storage</li><li>Latent Heat Storage</li><li>Thermochemical Storage</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Thermal Energy Storage Market Types can be categorized into three main types: Sensible Heat Storage, Latent Heat Storage, and Thermochemical Storage. Sensible Heat Storage involves storing and releasing thermal energy through temperature changes in a material. Latent Heat Storage, on the other hand, stores energy by utilizing phase change materials that absorb or release heat during their transition between solid and liquid states. Thermochemical Storage involves storing energy by using chemical reactions that produce heat when triggered. These three types offer different methods for efficiently storing and utilizing thermal energy.</p></p>
