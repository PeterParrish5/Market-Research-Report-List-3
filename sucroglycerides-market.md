<p><p>Sucroglycerides are a type of emulsifier that is derived from sugar and glycerol. There are different types of Sucroglycerides in the market, including Monoglycerides and Diglycerides. Monoglycerides have one glycerol molecule attached to a fatty acid chain, while Diglycerides have two glycerol molecules attached to a fatty acid chain. These emulsifiers are commonly used in food production to improve texture, stability, and shelf life of products. They are popular in a wide range of industries including food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics.</p></p>
