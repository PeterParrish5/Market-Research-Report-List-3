<p><p>MDF scraps waste is generated during the post-industrial processing of medium-density fiberboard. This waste can be classified into two market types based on the production process used: dry and wet. In the dry production process, MDF scraps are collected and utilized for applications that do not involve moisture, such as furniture making. In the wet production process, MDF scraps are repurposed for applications that involve moisture, such as moldings and trims in construction. Both market types serve to reduce waste and promote sustainability in manufacturing.</p></p>
